# Win/Loss Interview

A skill for designing, running, and synthesizing win/loss interviews into actionable insight.

## What this skill does

Provides the full system for win/loss programs:

1. Interview questionnaire (separate for closed-won and closed-lost)
2. Recruitment template (the email that gets customers to say yes)
3. Interview facilitation guide (how to run the 30-minute call)
4. Synthesis framework (turning N interviews into patterns)
5. Distribution plan (who gets what insight)
6. Action recommendations (what to change in product, sales, messaging)

## When to use

- Post-launch (T+30 to T+90)
- Quarterly as a standing program
- After losing a string of deals to a specific competitor
- When sales blames messaging and marketing blames sales (truth lives in customer voices)
- After a major positioning shift, to validate it landed

## How to run this skill

### Step 1: Establish scope

Ask:

- How many interviews? (Recommend 10 minimum, 5 closed-won and 5 closed-lost)
- Which time period? (Last quarter, last 6 months)
- Which segment or product line?
- Specific competitor focus, or general?
- Internal interviewer (PMM) or external firm? Internal is fine for most teams.

### Step 2: Recruit interviewees

The hardest part. Most customers won't respond. Tactics that work:

- Sales rep makes the intro personally (60%+ response rate)
- PMM emails directly with a specific ask (20% response rate)
- $50-100 gift card offered up front (raises response rate significantly)
- 25-minute call, not 30 (lower friction in calendar)

Recruitment email template:

```
Subject: Quick 25-min request — [Customer name]

Hi [Name],

[Sales rep name] mentioned you'd be a great person to talk to. We're doing a research project to learn from recent deals — both ones we won and ones we lost. I'd love 25 minutes of your time to hear your honest perspective.

What I'm not doing: pitching you anything, or trying to change your mind about a decision you already made.

What I am doing: trying to make our team better. Your candor will help us improve how we sell, what we build, and how we show up.

As a thank-you, I'll send a $100 [Gift card option]. Happy to share findings back with you when the study is complete.

Are you open to a quick call next week? I'm flexible — just send me 2-3 times that work.

Thanks,
[PMM name]
```

### Step 3: Run the interview

For closed-won customers, use this question set:

```
Opening (2 min)
- "Thanks for taking the time. Before we start, anything off-limits or sensitive?"

Context (5 min)
1. "Walk me back to the beginning. When did you first realize you had a problem worth solving?"
2. "What had you tried before evaluating us?"
3. "What triggered the search? What changed?"

Evaluation (10 min)
4. "Who else did you evaluate? How did you compare us to them?"
5. "What were the top 3 criteria you used to decide?"
6. "What almost stopped you from buying us?"
7. "Was there a specific moment or meeting that tipped you?"

Outcome (5 min)
8. "How would you describe what we do to a peer?"
9. "What's working well now that you're using us?"
10. "What would you tell a peer considering us?"

Close (3 min)
11. "If we could improve one thing, what would it be?"
12. "Anyone else on your team I should talk to?"
```

For closed-lost customers, use this question set:

```
Opening (2 min)
- "Thanks for being willing to share. To be clear, I'm not trying to win the deal back — I genuinely want to understand."

Context (5 min)
1. "Walk me back to when you started this search. What were you trying to solve?"
2. "What had you tried before?"

Evaluation (10 min)
3. "Who did you evaluate? Where did we land in your shortlist?"
4. "What were your top 3 decision criteria?"
5. "Where did we fall short for you?"
6. "Was there a specific moment we lost the deal?"
7. "What did the winner do that we didn't?"

Hypothetical (5 min)
8. "If you ran this evaluation again, would the outcome change? Why or why not?"
9. "Is there a scenario where you'd reconsider us?"
10. "What feedback would you give our sales team specifically?"

Close (3 min)
11. "Anything else I should know?"
12. "Mind if I follow up in 6 months?"
```

### Step 4: Synthesize patterns

After every 3-5 interviews, look for patterns:

- Repeated phrases (3+ customers using similar language)
- Consistent criteria (what shows up in everyone's top 3)
- Consistent gaps (what we lose on repeatedly)
- Consistent strengths (what we win on repeatedly)
- Surprising signals (something none of us expected)

Don't extrapolate from a single interview. Patterns require multiple voices saying the same thing in different ways.

### Step 6: Distribute insight

Different audiences need different formats:

- **Exec team:** 1-page synthesis with the 3 most important findings
- **Product team:** Product-specific recommendations with frequencies
- **Sales team:** What's working in pitch + what's not (specific moves)
- **Marketing team:** Quote bank + messaging implications
- **Everyone:** A monthly digest, not a one-time report

Set up a recurring cadence. Win/loss done once is wasted. Win/loss done monthly compounds.

## What good output looks like

- Patterns supported by multiple customer voices, not single interviews
- Direct customer language quoted, not paraphrased
- Action recommendations with named owners
- Honest about close calls and surprises
- Distribution plan that gets the right insight to the right people

## What to avoid

- Extrapolating from 1-2 interviews ("customers want X" based on one voice)
- Sanitizing tough feedback to protect feelings
- Making sales team feel attacked (they're partners, not the problem)
- Treating it as a one-time project. The value compounds with frequency.
- Skipping the closed-won interviews. Wins teach as much as losses.
